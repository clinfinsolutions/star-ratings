— Why this tool exists —

When Star Ratings and HAC penalties disagree, it's usually a data integrity story — not a quality story.

A meaningful share of PSI and HAC flags trace back to documentation accuracy, POA designation, and coding integrity — issues that drive both Star Ratings and HAC penalties, and are often correctable before the calculation period closes. AlignIQ™ is the framework I built to catch them.

— Data Sources & Methodology —
CMS Overall Hospital Star Rating
Sourced from publicly available CMS Provider data. The 2025 Release was published April 30, 2025 and reflects measure performance windows that vary by individual measure (typically 12–36 months ending 6 months prior to release). The 2026 Release is scheduled for May 13, 2026; this tool will display the comparison once the release is live.
CMS HAC Reduction Program
Data sourced from publicly available CMS Provider datasets. The Total HAC Score is calculated from PSI 90 (Patient Safety Indicator composite) and five hospital-acquired infection measures (CLABSI, CAUTI, SSI, CDI, MRSA). Total Hip/Knee Arthroplasty Complication Rate is a separate measure that contributes to the Safety of Care quartile but is not part of the HAC Reduction Program. Hospitals in the worst-performing HAC quartile receive a 1% Medicare payment reduction.

FY2025 HAC measurement windows: PSI 90 covers Jul 2021–Jun 2023; HAI measures cover Jan 2022–Dec 2023. FY2026 HAC measurement windows: PSI 90 covers Jul 2022–Jun 2024; HAI measures cover Jan 2023–Dec 2024. Hip/Knee reflects a 3-year rolling measurement window.
A note on correlation
Star Rating's Safety of Care measure group and the HAC Reduction Program use overlapping but not identical measure sets and statistical methods. The two are highly correlated but not equivalent: HAC penalty status is a strong predictor of Safety of Care quartile placement, but exceptions exist. Both PSI 90 + 5 HAIs (the HAC components) and Total Hip/Knee Arthroplasty Complication Rate contribute to the Safety of Care quartile that drives the new 2026 Star Rating 4-star cap.
